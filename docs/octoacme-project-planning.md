# OctoAcme — Project Planning

## Purpose
Turn an approved initiative into an actionable plan and backlog for delivery.

## Objectives
- Break work into shippable increments
- Identify dependencies and risks
- Align timelines, releases, and responsibilities

## Team Composition and Role Allocation

Choosing the right team composition at project kickoff reduces gaps in responsibility and improves delivery predictability.

### Essential vs. Optional Roles

| Role | Tier | When Required |
|---|---|---|
| Project Manager | Always Required | All projects |
| Product Manager | Always Required | All projects |
| Developers | Always Required | All projects |
| QA Lead | Usually Required | Any project with a formal testing or acceptance process |
| Technical Lead | Usually Required | Projects with significant architectural decisions or team mentorship needs |
| Scrum Master | Usually Required | Agile/scrum teams; especially valuable for larger or distributed teams |
| UX Designer | As Needed | Projects with user-facing features or significant UI/UX work |
| Business Analyst | As Needed | Projects with complex business rules or multiple stakeholder groups |

> **Example:** A typical project team includes PM, PdM, Developers, QA Lead, and Technical Lead. Scrum Master and UX Designer may be added for larger initiatives; Business Analyst is engaged when requirements are complex or stakeholder input is extensive.

### Role Assignment Checklist (Kickoff)
- [ ] Project Manager identified and confirmed
- [ ] Product Manager identified and confirmed
- [ ] Developers assigned with adequate capacity
- [ ] QA Lead identified (or QA responsibilities assigned to a Developer for small projects)
- [ ] Technical Lead identified if project requires architectural decisions
- [ ] Scrum Master confirmed for agile ceremonies
- [ ] UX Designer engaged if project has significant UI/UX scope
- [ ] Business Analyst engaged if requirements need formal elicitation or documentation

> See [Personas](octoacme-roles-and-personas.md) for detailed role definitions and responsibilities.

## Activities
1. Kickoff meeting with stakeholders and delivery team
2. Create prioritized backlog with acceptance criteria
3. Estimate scope (T-shirt sizing or story points)
4. Define Definition of Done (DoD)
5. Identify dependencies and integration points
6. Create release plan and milestone map

## Sprint / Iteration Planning
- Timebox planning to agreed sprint length
- Pull items that meet DoD and have clear acceptance criteria
- Ensure team capacity is respected
- Ensure all required roles are present in planning sessions and understand their responsibilities in the sprint

## Risk & Dependency Management
- Capture in Risk Register:
  - ID, Description, Impact, Probability, Owner, Mitigation
- Mark cross-team dependencies in the project board and escalate during weekly syncs

## Planning Checklist
- [ ] Project kickoff held
- [ ] Backlog prioritized and estimated
- [ ] Release timeline and milestones agreed
- [ ] Definition of Done documented
- [ ] Initial test plan / QA approach drafted
